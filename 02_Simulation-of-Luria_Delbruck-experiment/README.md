Lesson 02: Simulation of Luria-Delbrück experiment
=======================

The purpose of this lesson is to introduce students to the results of the original Luria-Delbrück experiment and have them perform a simulation of the growth of one generation of bacteria.

## Lesson Information
### Lesson Learning Outcomes
At the end of this lesson, students will be able to...
1. Represent bacterial populations computationally and simulate population growth through successive rounds of cell division.
2. Model mutation events within a growing population using Python data structures and random sampling techniques.
3. Explain how stochastic mutation events can influence the composition of bacterial populations and contribute to variability among replicate experiments.


### Content Prerequisites

Before beginning this lesson, students are expected to be familiar with the
following content topics:

* Lesson 01: Introduction to distributions

Also provided to support adopting instructors are
- `instructor-key.ipynb`: Instructor "key" notebook with completed code cells and full instructor commentary in Markdown cells
