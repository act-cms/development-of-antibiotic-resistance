Lesson 03: Selection-dependent mutation model
=======================

The purpose of this lesson is to show the students what a model of mutations arising following a certain stimulus (e.g., presence of antibiotic) would look like.

## Lesson Information
### Lesson Learning Outcomes
At the end of this lesson, students will be able to...
1. Develop a quantitative model predicting mutant frequencies if resistance mutations arise only after exposure to selective pressure.
2. Use Poisson-based statistical methods to estimate mutation rates from experimental observations.
3. Critically assess whether observed experimental data are consistent with a selection-dependent model of mutation.


### Content Prerequisites

Before beginning this lesson, students are expected to be familiar with the
following content topics:

* Lesson 02: Simulation of Luria-Delbrück experiment.

### Additional files
Also provided to support adopting instructors are
- `weitz_pois.csv`: Mock dataset that obeys the Poisson distribution, as presented in the [Weitz textbook](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691181509/quantitative-biosciences).
