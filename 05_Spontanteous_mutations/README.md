Lesson 05: Spontaneous mutations
=======================

The purpose of this lesson is to provide the mathematical foundations needed to estimate the timing of mutation appearance.

## Lesson Information
### Lesson Learning Outcomes
At the end of this lesson, students will be able to...
1. Distinguish between Darwinian (spontaneous) and Lamarckian (selection-dependent) explanations for the origin of antibiotic resistance.
2. Explain the biological assumptions underlying the spontaneous-mutation hypothesis and how it differs from the selection-dependent model.
3. Construct and interpret mathematical models describing the simultaneous growth of susceptible and resistant bacterial populations.
4. Predict how the timing of spontaneous mutations influences the final number of resistant cells observed after selection.


### Content Prerequisites

Before beginning this lesson, students are expected to be familiar with the
following content topics:

* Lesson 04: Modeling the growth of mutants
